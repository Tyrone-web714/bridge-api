# Volume III — Fleet Operations, Navigation & Logistics Intelligence

## Purpose

This volume governs driver workflows, supervisor workflows, routes, stops, customer intelligence, fleet visibility, dispatch support, commercial navigation, and offline field execution.

## Driver application

The driver app exists to improve safety and field execution.

Driver interaction while moving must be minimized.

Voice interaction should be preferred where practical.

Offline operation must support:

- Navigation
- Stop completion
- Notes
- Photos
- Safety reports
- Route progress
- Automatic sync on reconnection

## Navigation

Truck-aware routing must consider:

- Vehicle height
- Vehicle weight
- Vehicle length
- Axles
- Low bridges
- Truck restrictions
- Weight restrictions
- No-through-truck roads
- Construction
- Traffic
- Road closures
- Shared hazards

Safety takes priority over shortest distance.

## Routes

Routes include ordered Stops and lifecycle states:

- Planned
- Assigned
- Active
- Paused
- Completed
- Cancelled
- Archived

Completed route history should remain immutable except for documented administrative correction.

## Stops

Each Stop may include:

- Customer
- Location
- Scheduled time
- Arrival time
- Departure time
- Status
- Photos
- Driver notes
- Supervisor notes
- Safety notes
- Proof of delivery

## Customer intelligence

Customer records become operational knowledge repositories.

They may include:

- Preferred entrance
- Dock information
- Parking guidance
- Hazards
- Delivery instructions
- Photos/videos
- Access restrictions
- Hours
- Emergency contacts

## Supervisor operations

Supervisor dashboards must surface:

- Driver status
- Route status
- Late deliveries
- Safety alerts
- Vehicle locations
- Exceptions
- Communication queue
- Operational KPIs

## Route memory

The platform should maintain route memory for replay, comparison, optimization, training, and AI analysis.
