# Volume IV — Business Intelligence, KPI Engine, Analytics & Artificial Intelligence

## Purpose

This volume governs Business Intelligence, custom KPIs, formula engine, dashboards, reporting, predictive analytics, prescriptive analytics, AI insights, executive scorecards, and geographic intelligence.

## BI philosophy

Every Organization decides which metrics matter most.

The platform must not force all Organizations to use the same dashboard.

## KPI engine

Organizations can create unlimited KPIs without software changes.

Each KPI should support:

- Name
- Description
- Category
- Formula
- Data source
- Unit
- Goal
- Warning threshold
- Critical threshold
- Refresh interval
- Visibility
- Permissions
- Effective date
- Version

## Formula engine

Supported functions:

- SUM
- AVG
- COUNT
- MIN
- MAX
- MEDIAN
- PERCENTAGE
- RATIO
- TIME DIFFERENCE
- ROLLING AVERAGE
- WEIGHTED AVERAGE
- CUSTOM EXPRESSIONS

Formula versioning is mandatory so historical reports remain accurate.

## Dashboards

Dashboards may include:

- KPI cards
- Line charts
- Bar charts
- Pie charts
- Tables
- Maps
- Heat maps
- Leaderboards
- Forecast cards
- AI insight cards
- Scorecards

## Drill-down hierarchy

Organization → Region → Depot → Supervisor → Driver → Vehicle → Route → Stop → Customer

## AI

AI may generate:

- Executive summaries
- Operational summaries
- Safety summaries
- Supervisor coaching
- Driver coaching
- Cost reduction suggestions
- Efficiency recommendations
- Risk predictions

AI must never execute operational changes without human approval.

## AI explainability

Every AI recommendation should include:

- Confidence score
- Contributing factors
- Data sources
- Reasoning summary
- Timestamp
- Model version
